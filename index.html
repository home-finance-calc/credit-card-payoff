Frequently Asked Questions

How is credit card interest calculated? +

Credit card interest is typically calculated using your Average Daily Balance (ADB). Your APR is divided by 365 to get the daily rate, which is then applied to your balance each day. At the end of the billing cycle, these daily interest charges are summed and added to your balance.

What happens if I only pay the minimum? +

Paying only the minimum‚Äîtypically 1-3% of your balance‚Äîmeans most of your payment goes toward interest. A $5,000 balance at 20% APR with minimum payments could take over 20 years to pay off and cost you more than $8,000 in interest alone.

How can I pay off my credit card faster? +

Pay more than the minimum whenever possible. Consider balance transfer cards with 0% intro APR, the debt avalanche method (paying highest interest first), or the debt snowball method (paying smallest balances first for psychological wins).

Is this calculator accurate? +

Our calculator provides accurate estimates based on standard compound interest formulas. Actual results may vary slightly due to billing cycle timing, additional charges, payment posting dates, and variable APR changes. Always verify with your credit card statement.
